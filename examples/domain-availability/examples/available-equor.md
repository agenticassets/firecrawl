Tips for choosing a domain

## What makes a great domain name?

### Choosing the right domain is critical for your website. When you combine it with the right extension, it becomes even more powerful.

### Relevant

Your domain name should connect to your brand, business, and web content. It should give visitors a clear idea of what to expect when they land on your site.

### Memorable

Great domain names stick in people's minds and stand out from the crowd. Avoid complex or confusing words that challenge spelling.

### Short

Short domains are easier to remember and type. Avoid unnecessary words or hyphens that can make your domain name hard to remember.

### On-brand

Your domain name and extension offer an opportunity to reinforce your brand identity. Extensions and TLDs specific to your industry can enhance the relevance and credibility of your website.

## Domain Name Search FAQs

### What is a domain name?

A domain name is the address you type into your web browser to visit a website, like "instantdomainsearch.com." It identifies a website on the internet, making it easy for people to find and remember. Like your home address helps people find where you live, a domain name helps people find your website. Our domain search tool helps you find available domains.

### What if the domain name I want is already taken?

If someone already owns the domain name you want, you still have several options:

- **Try a different extension:** if someone owns "example.com," check if "example.net," "example.org," or other TLDs remain available. See the full [list of available extensions](https://instantdomainsearch.com/domain-extensions).
- **Create a variation:** add a word, hyphen, or change the order of words to create a variation of your desired domain name. Our [Domain Name Generator](https://instantdomainsearch.com/domain-generator) suggests similar available domains. Our [premium domain search tool](https://instantdomainsearch.com/premium-domains) also helps you find similar, high-performing domains.
- **Contact the owner:** the current owner might sell their domain. You can often find owner information by visiting the domain or using WHOIS lookup to locate the owner.

### How do I check if a domain name is available?

Use our [domain checker](https://instantdomainsearch.com/) to see if a domain name is available. Type the name you want, and you'll receive real-time results that check domains across different extensions (like ".com" or ".net") and provide alternative suggestions.

### What are extensions and TLDs, and which one should I choose?

A TLD (Top-Level Domain) represents the part of a domain name that comes after the dot, like ".com" or ".org." The .com extension ranks as the most common, but many others, such as ".net" or industry-specific extensions like ".tech" or ".shop," offer great alternatives.
