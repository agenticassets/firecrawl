How to Keep Your apartment safe during holiday travel
-----------------------------------------------------

**December 5, 2025**

The holiday season is a time for celebration, travel, and making memories, but it’s also a time when apartments can be more vulnerable to accidents or break-ins. Whether you’re heading out of town for a weekend or spending several weeks away, a little preparation goes a long way in keeping your home safe and giving you peace of mind.

At **First Commercial Real Estate & Management**, your comfort and security matter to us. Here are our top tips to help ensure your apartment stays safe while you enjoy your holiday travels

### **Double-Check Doors, Windows & Locks**

Before you leave, take a few minutes to do a walk-through:

*   Ensure all windows are fully closed and locked
    
*   Test your front-door lock and deadbolt
    
*   Remove spare keys stored outside—thieves know the common hiding spots
    
*   If you have a patio or balcony door, secure it with the provided locking bar or rod
    

A quick security sweep can eliminate many potential risks.

* * *

### **Use Smart Lighting or Timers**

A dark, quiet apartment for several days can signal that no one is home. To help your home look lived-in:

*   Set a few lamps on timers
    
*   Use smart bulbs or plugs to automate lighting
    
*   Leave blinds or curtains in their usual positions
    

Consistency is key—don’t make sudden changes right before leaving.

* * *

### **Unplug Nonessential Electronics**

Beyond security, safety matters too. Reduce fire risk and save energy by unplugging:

*   Small appliances
    
*   TVs and gaming consoles
    
*   Chargers
    
*   Holiday lights
    

This simple step helps prevent electrical hazards while you’re gone.

* * *

### **Hold Your Mail and Deliveries**

Overflowing mail or stacks of packages outside your door are clear signs you’re not home.  
Options include:

*   Placing a USPS mail hold
    
*   Using Amazon Locker or package rooms (if available at your property)
    
*   Asking a trusted neighbor to pick up deliveries
    

* * *

### **Avoid Broadcasting Your Travel Plans**

It’s tempting to share vacation photos in real time, but doing so can unintentionally alert others that your home is empty.  
Take photos—just wait to post them until you’re back.

* * *

### **Adjust Your Thermostat Responsibly**

If you’ll be gone for an extended period:

*   Set your thermostat to an energy-efficient but safe temperature
    
*   In cold climates, make sure heat stays above the minimum recommended level to prevent frozen pipes
    

* * *

### **Remove Garbage & Perishable Food**

Before leaving, clear your fridge and take out the trash. Nothing welcomes you home like… well, not bad smells.

* * *

### **Secure Valuables**

Store small valuable items—passports, jewelry, important documents—in a secure place. A small lockbox can offer extra peace of mind.

* * *

### **Rely on Your Community**

One of the best things about multifamily living is having neighbors nearby.  
Consider:

*   Letting a trusted neighbor know you’ll be gone
    
*   Asking them to report anything unusual
    

* * *

### **Enjoy Your Holiday Season with Confidence**

With a few thoughtful steps, you can head out for the holidays knowing your apartment is secure. From all of us at **First Commercial Real Estate & Management**, we hope you have a safe, warm, and joyful holiday season!

What Are Rent Concessions and Why Are They Beneficial?
------------------------------------------------------

**September 2, 2025**

If you’ve been searching for an apartment, you may have come across the term **“rent concession.”** But what exactly does that mean, and how can it benefit both renters and property owners?

### What Are Rent Concessions?

A **rent concession** is an incentive offered by a landlord or property management company to attract new tenants or encourage current residents to renew their lease. Concessions typically involve temporary discounts or perks that make leasing more appealing.

Common types of rent concessions include:

*   **Reduced Rent:** For example, “First month free” or “$200 off your first three months.”
    
*   **Waived Fees:** Application, parking, or amenity fees may be temporarily eliminated.
    
*   **Added Perks:** Free upgrades, gift cards, or additional amenities at no extra cost.
    

### Why Rent Concessions Are Beneficial

### For Renters:

*   **Immediate Savings:** Concessions can lower move-in costs, which is especially helpful during times when money is tight.
    
*   **Flexibility:** Short-term discounts allow renters to experience a new community without a long-term financial strain.
    
*   **Access to Better Housing:** Incentives may open the door to communities or apartments that might otherwise be out of budget.
    

### For Property Owners & Managers:

*   **Attract New Residents Quickly:** Offering a concession makes a property stand out in a competitive market.
    
*   **Reduce Vacancy Rates:** The faster units are filled, the less income is lost.
    
*   **Encourage Renewals:** A well-timed concession can help retain happy residents.
    

### Final Thoughts

Rent concessions can be a win-win: renters enjoy cost savings and perks, while property owners benefit from lower vacancies and satisfied residents. Whether it’s a discount on rent, waived fees, or a unique perk, these incentives create added value and make leasing more attractive.

If you’re in the market for a new place, keep an eye out for rent concessions—they might just help you land the perfect home at the perfect price.

What First-Time Multifamily Investors Should Know
-------------------------------------------------

**August 18, 2025**

Investing in multifamily properties — whether it’s a small apartment building or a larger community — can be an exciting way to grow wealth, generate steady income, and diversify your portfolio. But like any investment, it comes with its own learning curve. If you’re considering your first step into multifamily real estate, here are some key things you should know.

### 1\. Understand the Appeal of Multifamily Investments

Unlike single-family rentals, multifamily properties provide multiple streams of income under one roof. Even if one unit is vacant, the others can continue generating revenue. Multifamily properties also tend to appreciate steadily and may offer tax advantages that make them especially attractive to long-term investors.

* * *

### 2\. Financing is Different Than Single-Family

While many first-time investors are familiar with conventional home loans, multifamily properties often require different financing structures. For smaller properties (2–4 units), you may still be able to use a residential loan. Larger properties (5+ units) usually involve commercial financing, with stricter requirements for down payments, reserves, and borrower experience. It’s important to connect with a lender who specializes in multifamily lending early in the process.

* * *

### 3\. Location Matters More Than You Think

A multifamily property’s success hinges on its location. Look for areas with strong rental demand, job growth, and access to amenities like schools, retail, and transportation. A great building in the wrong neighborhood won’t perform as well as a modest property in a thriving area.

* * *

### 4\. Know Your Numbers

Successful multifamily investing is all about the math. Before making an offer, you’ll want to analyze:

*   **Net Operating Income (NOI):** Revenue minus operating expenses.
    
*   **Cap Rate:** A measure of the property’s potential return.
    
*   **Cash-on-Cash Return:** How much income you’re earning compared to the cash you invested.
    
*   **Vacancy Rates & Rent Growth:** Local market data that impacts long-term performance.
    

* * *

### 5\. Property Management Can Make or Break You

Multifamily properties require consistent oversight — from leasing and maintenance to tenant relations and compliance. Many first-time investors underestimate the time commitment. Partnering with an experienced property management company can protect your investment, keep residents satisfied, and free you up to focus on strategy instead of daily operations.

* * *

### 6\. Expect the Unexpected

Vacancies, maintenance surprises, and market shifts are part of the investment landscape. Smart investors plan ahead by setting aside reserves for repairs and turnover, ensuring they aren’t caught off guard by unexpected expenses.

* * *

### 7\. Think Long-Term

Multifamily properties are rarely a “get-rich-quick” investment. Wealth is typically built over time, through consistent cash flow, gradual rent increases, and property appreciation. Going in with a long-term mindset will help you weather short-term challenges and stay focused on your financial goals.

### Final Thoughts

Multifamily investing can be a rewarding path to financial independence, but first-time investors should take the time to educate themselves, build a strong team of advisors, and choose their property wisely. With the right preparation and support, your first multifamily investment can be the foundation of a successful real estate portfolio.

If you’re ready to take the next step in multifamily investing, **don’t go it alone — partner with experts who know how to maximize your returns and protect your investment.**

state of the multifamily market: first half of 2025


-----------------------------------------------------

June 17, 2025

Demand > Supply = Rents rising! 

Q1 2025: absorption highest since ’85, vacancy at ~4.8%, investor interest surging. Landlord-friendly times ahead. Dive into the H1 multifamily trends:

### 1\. **Rising Demand Amid Slowing Supply**

*   Net absorption surged to approximately **100,600 units in Q1**, a 77% jump year-over-year, pushing vacancy rates down to **4.8%**, the lowest first-quarter level since 1985.
    
*   Meanwhile, **new deliveries slowed**: developers delivered around **116,000 units in Q1**, down 27% from Q3 2024 and 6.8% year-over-year.
    

### 2\. **Rent Growth Remains Steady**

*   Average asking rents rose by roughly **1.0% YoY**, reaching around **$1,761–$1,755** nationally by March–May across multifamily and single-family rental portfolios.
    
*   Smaller metro markets, especially the **Midwest and Northeast**, led rent gains—markets like NYC soared ~5.5–5.7%, Kansas City 4.0%, Columbus ~3.3%.
    

### 3\. **Investors Regain Confidence**

*   Multifamily investment activity picked up, with **$157 billion in sales over 12 months**, and Q1 2025 investment sales rose **9.7% over Q1 2024**, accounting for **36.1%** of total U.S. CRE volume.

🕵️‍♂️ How Identity Theft Happens in Multifamily Communities

**1\. Fraudulent Rental Applications**  
Individuals may use stolen identities or forged documents to secure a lease. This can lead to skipped rent, criminal activity on-site, and legal complications for property managers.

**2\. Mail Theft**  
Communal mailboxes and unsecured package delivery areas can be easy targets for identity thieves looking for credit card statements, bank information, or pre-approved loan offers.

**3\. Data Breaches & Cybersecurity Lapses**  
Multifamily operators store sensitive information—like social security numbers, driver’s licenses, and financial records. If software systems aren’t secure or staff aren’t trained properly, this data can be vulnerable.

**4\. Phishing & Social Engineering**  
Residents and staff may unknowingly fall victim to scams that trick them into revealing personal information via phone, email, or even in-person deception.

* * *

### 🛠️ Steps Management Can Take

**1\. Strengthen Application Screening**

*   Use a reputable tenant screening service.
    
*   Cross-check documentation and verify employment and rental history thoroughly.
    
*   Be cautious of inconsistencies in IDs or incomplete applications.
    

**2\. Secure Mail & Packages**

*   Encourage residents to pick up mail promptly.
    
*   Install secure, lockable mailboxes and package lockers.
    
*   Work with postal carriers or delivery services to limit unattended drop-offs.
    

**3\. Invest in Data Protection**

*   Use encrypted property management software.
    
*   Regularly update passwords and software systems.
    
*   Limit access to sensitive data based on employee roles.
    

**4\. Educate Your Team & Residents**

*   Train staff to spot suspicious behavior or documents.
    
*   Send regular reminders to residents about mail theft, phishing scams, and online safety.
    
*   Provide resources on reporting fraud and protecting personal information.
    

* * *

### 👮 What To Do If Identity Theft Is Suspected

If identity theft is suspected on your property:

1.  **Document the incident** – gather all relevant information and evidence.
    
2.  **Alert authorities** – contact local law enforcement and, if needed, the U.S. Postal Inspection Service.
    
3.  **Notify affected parties** – including residents, vendors, or staff who may be impacted.
    
4.  **Audit your systems** – assess any potential weaknesses and take corrective action.
    
5.  **Cooperate with investigations** – provide support to police or legal teams as needed.

Many renters believe their landlord’s insurance policy will cover them in case of fire, theft, or damage. Unfortunately, that’s not the case. A landlord’s policy typically only covers the building itself—not your personal belongings, liability, or temporary living expenses if disaster strikes.

That’s where **renters insurance** comes in.

### 🏠 What Is Renters Insurance?

Renters insurance is a policy that protects your personal property and offers liability coverage while you’re renting a home or apartment. For a small monthly premium—usually between $10 and $25—you gain peace of mind and financial protection.

* * *

### 🔒 What Does It Cover?

Most standard policies include:

**1\. Personal Property Protection**  
If your belongings are damaged or stolen due to events like fire, theft, vandalism, or certain types of water damage, renters insurance helps you replace them.

**2\. Liability Coverage**  
If someone is injured in your unit or you accidentally damage another person’s property (like a leak that damages your neighbor’s ceiling), this coverage can help pay for legal and medical costs.

**3\. Additional Living Expenses (ALE)**  
If your apartment becomes uninhabitable due to a covered event, your policy may pay for hotel stays, food, and other costs while you’re temporarily displaced.

* * *

### 💸 Why It’s Worth Every Penny

**It’s affordable** – For less than the cost of a streaming subscription, you can protect thousands of dollars’ worth of personal property.

**It can save you from major out-of-pocket expenses** – Without coverage, you’d have to replace everything you own on your own, not to mention pay for alternative housing in an emergency.

**It helps protect your future** – Having renters insurance and filing claims responsibly can positively affect your insurance history, potentially lowering premiums for future homeowners or auto policies.

* * *

### 🧾 Renters Insurance & Your Lease

Some apartment communities require renters insurance as part of the lease agreement, and for good reason: it helps protect both you and the property. Even if it’s not mandatory, it’s still highly recommended as part of being a responsible resident.

* * *

### ✅ How to Get Started

*   Shop around: Compare quotes from reputable providers.
    
*   Know what you need: Take an inventory of your belongings.
    
*   Understand your policy: Make sure it covers the types of risks most likely to affect you (like floods, if you’re in a flood-prone area).
    

* * *

### Final Thought

Accidents and disasters are unpredictable—but your financial well-being doesn’t have to be. Renters insurance is a small investment with big benefits. Whether you’re in a studio apartment or a three-bedroom rental, it’s one of the easiest ways to protect yourself, your belongings, and your peace of mind.

Storm & Severe Weather Safety: A Guide for Apartment Living


---------------------------------------------------------------

May 19, 2025

Whether it’s tornado season in Oklahoma, heavy thunderstorms, or unexpected winter storms, it’s crucial to be prepared—and to know what actions to take when weather turns dangerous.

Here’s how apartment residents can stay safe and informed when storms strike:

### 🌪️ **1\. Know Your Risks**

Tulsa and many areas in Oklahoma are prone to:

*   Tornadoes and straight-line winds
    
*   Severe thunderstorms with hail and lightning
    
*   Flash flooding
    
*   Power outages
    

Check local emergency management websites or apps like FEMA, Red Cross, or your local news station for area-specific alerts.

* * *

### 📲 **2\. Sign Up for Weather Alerts**

Make sure you’re receiving emergency alerts. Here’s how:

*   Enable Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEAs) on your smartphone.
    
*   Download a reliable weather app (such as NOAA Weather Radar or WeatherBug).
    
*   Follow your city’s emergency management on social media for updates.
    

* * *

### 🧰 **3\. Prepare a Basic Emergency Kit**

Even in an apartment, you can keep a compact emergency kit ready:

*   Flashlight & extra batteries
    
*   First aid kit
    
*   Bottled water (1 gallon per person per day, at least 3 days)
    
*   Non-perishable snacks
    
*   Medications and essential documents
    
*   Phone charger & power bank
    
*   Whistle (in case of rescue needs)
    
*   Extra face masks and hand sanitizer
    

Store your kit in a location that’s easy to grab in a hurry.

* * *

### 🧭 **4\. Know Where to Shelter**

In an apartment:

*   **Ground floor units** or **interior bathrooms** are usually safest.
    
*   Avoid windows and exterior walls.
    
*   If your building has a designated storm shelter or safe room, **know where it is ahead of time**.
    
*   Live on a higher floor? Ask management if there is a safer area in the building to relocate during storms.
    

* * *

### 🔌 **5\. Before the Storm Hits**

*   Charge your phone and devices.
    
*   Bring pets indoors and gather their supplies.
    
*   Move patio furniture or loose items from balconies to prevent them from becoming projectiles.
    
*   Fill the bathtub with water for non-drinking use if flooding or water supply issues are possible.
    

* * *

### 💡 **6\. During the Storm**

*   Stay indoors and away from windows.
    
*   Monitor the situation with a weather radio or app.
    
*   Avoid elevators, especially during tornado watches or power outage threats.
    
*   If flooding begins, move to higher ground _safely_ and avoid walking through water.
    

* * *

### 🛠️ **7\. After the Storm**

*   Wait for official confirmation that it’s safe to leave your shelter.
    
*   Report damage or hazards (like gas smells or downed lines) to your property manager immediately.
    
*   Be cautious of debris, broken glass, and slick areas in common spaces.
    
*   Take photos of any damage to your belongings for insurance purposes.
    

* * *

### Severe weather can happen quickly, but with preparation and awareness, you can weather the storm safely—even from your apartment. Talk with us about building-specific safety plans, and consider sharing this information with neighbors to help foster a safe and informed community.

Stay safe, stay ready—and don’t wait until the storm sirens sound to make your plan.

**Understanding fair housing law: what every property owner needs to know**


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

April 9, 2025

The **Fair Housing Act**, originally passed in 1968 and amended over the years, prohibits discrimination in the sale, rental, and financing of housing based on: 

*   Race
*   Color
*   National Origin
*   Religion

*   Sex
*   Familial Status
*   Disability

Some states and municipalities may include additional protections, such as source of income, age, or military status, so it’s important to be familiar with both **federal and local laws**.

### **Why It Matters for Property Owners**

Violating Fair Housing Laws can result in **costly lawsuits**, **fines**, and **damage to your reputation**. Even unintentional discrimination—such as writing a rental ad that includes phrasing like “perfect for singles”—can be considered a violation. It’s your responsibility as a landlord or property manager to treat all prospective and current tenants equally and fairly.

### **Best Practices for Compliance**

To stay in compliance and foster an inclusive rental environment, follow these guidelines:

#### 1\. **Standardize Your Screening Process**

Use the same criteria for all applicants, such as income-to-rent ratio, credit score, background checks, and rental history. Avoid subjective language like “gut feeling.”

#### 2\. **Be Careful with Advertising**

Your rental listings should focus on the property, not the type of tenant you’re looking for. Avoid phrases that suggest preference, such as “ideal for young couples” or “no kids.”

#### 3\. **Provide Reasonable Accommodations**

If a tenant has a disability, be prepared to make reasonable modifications or accommodations, such as allowing a service animal even if you have a no-pets policy.

#### 4\. **Train Your Staff**

If you work with a team, make sure everyone involved in leasing or property management is trained on Fair Housing Laws and understands how to avoid discrimination—intentional or not.

#### 5\. **Document Everything**

Keep written records of interactions with applicants and tenants, especially decisions about applications or lease terminations. Clear documentation can help protect you if questions arise.

### **Work With Professionals**

Partnering with a knowledgeable property management company like First Commercial can make a big difference in maintaining compliance. We stay up to date on changing laws and can help you navigate complex situations with confidence.
